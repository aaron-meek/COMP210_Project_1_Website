Terms

Information on terms are inspired by the book Understanding Computers in a Changing Society (6th edition) by Deborah Morley.


Here are twenty general terms I find to be quite helpful to understand and be aware of.

Assistive technology

Paraphrase - Assistive technology (AT) is any item, piece of equipment, software program, or product system that is used to increase, maintain, or improve the functional capabilities of persons with disabilities


Cloud storage

There many forms of storage systems, and methods for storing data. Cloud storage is a form of remote or online storage. The idea of cloud storage is that data can be securely transfered to, and stored in, remote data centers. Data stored in remote data centers can also be viewed by, managed with, and downloaded to a remote personal/company computer through the power of the internet. You can make your own remote server to act as cloud storage, but many businesses and individuals will opt for cloud storage serveses like Dropbox, and other cloud computing serveses.


Computer literacy

Computer literacy is a non-rigourous measurement of a persons knowledge and understand of computer concepts and functionalities. Some examples of basic computer concepts and functionalities include

  • Knowing high lever computer parts like the CPU, GPU, RAM, PSU, and etc.
  • Understanding what computer software is and how to use it
  • Unserstanding how to use the internet
  • Understanding that some older computers can be revitalized through component upgrades


Digital manipulation

Ethical issues also exist in the realm of computers. One issue pertans to how easily digital media can be altered and transformed in such a way that may be damaging to the content creater, or designed missinform the reader. Preserved data of late actor Peter Cushing was used in production of computer generated graphics for the purpose of having him appear in the movie Star Wars: Rogue One. Clear applications of digital manipulation exist, but whether it is ethical and safe is still subject to debate.


E-portfolio

An e-portfolio is an electronic portfolio. More secifically, it is a portfolio that is accessible online. If you are an artist, then a good method to increasing exposure to your work and talents is to create an online portfolio for others to view. Some downsides to posting your work online is that it may be easily plagarized. However, potential employers can quickly look at the sorts of projects you've worked on, and guage experience and work qualities you may possess.


Ergonomic hardware

With the aid of computers, we find ourselves constantly surrounded with them. Many jobs require the prolonged use of computers, and with prolonged use comes several health risks. A study on the ergonomic deficiencies of computer workstations in Bangladeshi University found that there were associated health risks with prolonged use of computers, (Kibria et al, 2023). It is important to use ergonomic accessories when planning to perform work for lengthy periods. Many retailers that sell computer accessories such as mice, keyboards, and office chairs also sell ergonomic forms.


Identity theft

Before the advent of the World Wide Web, identity theaft often required one to physically obtain another's proof of identity. Presently, the majority of people have some form of information stored on an external server. Your doctor's office holds the files of many people, each containing private information. Many of these same people have accounts with online businesses tied to their payment information, and the security of said account may depend on the private information found on a person's file. Bad actors often target these data centers (big or small) to obtain your data. They may proceed to sell the information, or use it directly. Modern technology has enabled these sorts of events to take place between multiple parties across the globe. In a modern world it is important to develop safe online habits, and for large companies to take security seriously.
